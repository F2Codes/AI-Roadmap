# 🧠 Chapter 1 — What is Artificial Intelligence?

Before we jump into coding, algorithms, and neural networks, let's answer the most important question:

> **What is Artificial Intelligence?**

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a field of computer science that focuses on creating systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence.

These tasks include:

- 🧠 Learning from data
- 💬 Understanding human language
- 👀 Recognizing images and videos
- 🎙️ Understanding speech
- ✍️ Generating text
- 🎨 Creating images
- 🤖 Making decisions
- 📈 Finding patterns in data

Today, AI is almost everywhere.

You use AI when:

- 📱 Your phone unlocks using Face ID.
- 🎬 Netflix recommends movies you might enjoy.
- 🛒 Amazon suggests products based on your interests.
- 🌍 Google Translate helps you communicate across languages.
- 💬 ChatGPT answers your questions.
- 🚗 Self-driving cars detect roads, signs, and obstacles.

> **AI isn't magic.**

It's a combination of **mathematics, statistics, programming, and data**. Every AI application you see today is built upon these foundations.

# ❓ Do You Need to Be a Genius?

Absolutely **not**.

One of the biggest myths about AI is that only math geniuses or PhD researchers can learn it.

The truth is much simpler.

If you're willing to learn, practice consistently, and enjoy solving problems, you can absolutely become an AI engineer.

Nobody starts as an expert.

Every professional AI engineer once wrote their very first line of code.
